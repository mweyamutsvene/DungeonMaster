## Barbarian

### Core Barbarian Traits

| Primary Ability | Hit Point Die | Saving Throw Proficiencies | Skill Proficiencies | Weapon Proficiencies | Armor Training | Starting Equipment |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strength | D12 per Barbarian level | Strength and Constitution | Choose 2: Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Survival | Simple and Martial weapons | Light and Medium armor and Shields | Choose A or B: (A) Greataxe, 4 Handaxes, Explorer’s Pack, 15 GP; or (B) 75 GP |

Barbarians are mighty warriors powered by primal forces that manifest as Rage. Their Rage grants extraordinary might and resilience and can represent a spirit, ancestral force, or raw elemental fury.

### Becoming a Barbarian

**As a Level 1 Character**
- Gain the Core Barbarian Traits.
- Gain the Barbarian’s level 1 features (see Barbarian Features table).

**As a Multiclass Character**
- Gain Hit Point Die, proficiency with Martial weapons, and training with Shields from Core Barbarian Traits.
- Gain the Barbarian’s level 1 features.

##### Level 1: Rage
- Enter Rage as a Bonus Action if you aren’t wearing Heavy armor.
- While Raging: Resistance to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing; bonus to Strength attacks’ damage; Advantage on Strength checks and saves; can’t concentrate or cast spells.
- Duration: until end of your next turn; extend by attacking, forcing a save, or using a Bonus Action. Max duration up to 10 minutes.

##### Level 1: Unarmored Defense
- While not wearing armor, base AC = 10 + Dexterity modifier + Constitution modifier. You can use a Shield.

##### Level 1: Weapon Mastery
- Gain mastery properties for two chosen Simple or Martial melee weapons; can change one after a Long Rest.

##### Level 2: Danger Sense
- Advantage on Dexterity saving throws unless Incapacitated.

##### Level 2: Reckless Attack
- On your first attack roll each turn, you may attack recklessly to gain Advantage on Strength attack rolls until your next turn, but attacks against you have Advantage.

##### Level 3: Barbarian Subclass
- Choose a subclass (e.g., Path of the Berserker).

##### Level 3: Primal Knowledge
- Gain proficiency in one additional skill from the Barbarian skill list. While Raging, you can use Strength for certain skill checks (Acrobatics, Intimidation, Perception, Stealth, Survival).

##### Path of the Berserker — Subclass Features
**Channel Rage into Violent Fury.** Barbarians who walk the Path of the Berserker direct their Rage primarily toward violence.

###### Level 3: Frenzy
- If you use Reckless Attack while your Rage is active, the first target you hit on your turn takes extra damage equal to the sum of a number of d6s equal to your Rage Damage bonus; the damage has the same type as the weapon or Unarmed Strike.

###### Level 6: Mindless Rage
- While your Rage is active, you have Immunity to the Charmed and Frightened conditions. If you are Charmed or Frightened when you enter Rage, that condition ends.

###### Level 10: Retaliation
- When you take damage from a creature within 5 feet of you, you can use your Reaction to make one melee attack (weapon or Unarmed Strike) against that creature.

###### Level 14: Intimidating Presence
- As a Bonus Action, choose creatures in a 30-foot emanation; each must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + Strength modifier + Proficiency Bonus) or become Frightened for 1 minute. The frightened creature repeats the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success.
- Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a Long Rest unless you expend a use of your Rage to restore it.
