## Bard

### Core Bard Traits

| Primary Ability | Hit Point Die | Saving Throw Proficiencies | Skill Proficiencies | Weapon Proficiencies | Tool Proficiencies | Armor Training | Starting Equipment |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Charisma | D8 per Bard level | Dexterity and Charisma | Choose any 3 skills | Simple weapons | Choose 3 Musical Instruments | Light armor | Choose A or B: (A) Leather, 2 Daggers, Musical Instrument, Entertainer’s Pack, 19 GP; or (B) 90 GP |

Bards invoke magic through music, dance, and verse. They inspire, heal, dismay, and beguile, drawing on the Words of Creation to shape magic that transcends language.

### Becoming a Bard

**As a Level 1 Bard**
- Gain the Core Bard Traits.
- Gain Bard level 1 features (see Bard Features table).

**As a Multiclass Bard**
- Gain Hit Die, proficiency in one skill, one musical instrument, and Light armor training.
- Gain Bard level 1 features; consult multiclass spell slot rules.

### Bard Class Features

(See Bard Features table for an overview of levels, Bardic Die, cantrips, prepared spells, and spell slots.)

**Notable features (short):**
- Bardic Inspiration (gives allies bonus dice that scale with level)
- Spellcasting (Charisma-based spells)
- Expertise (double Proficiency Bonus for two skills)
- Jack of All Trades (add half proficiency to nonproficient skill checks)
- Font of Inspiration (Bardic Inspiration recharges on a short rest at level 5)
- Countercharm (resist being charmed or frightened in an emanation)
- Magical Secrets (learn spells from any class; appears at higher levels)
- Superior Inspiration (at level 18, regain Bardic Inspiration use when you roll Initiative)

##### Level 1: Bardic Inspiration
- As a Bonus Action, grant a creature within 60 feet that can see or hear you one Bardic Inspiration die (d6 at level 1). The creature can add it to one D20 Test roll within 1 hour. You can use this a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1), regaining uses on a Long Rest. The die size increases at higher Bard levels: d8 (level 5), d10 (level 10), d12 (level 15).

##### Level 1: Spellcasting
- You know cantrips and prepare spells from the Bard spell list. You regain expended spell slots on a Long Rest. Charisma is your spellcasting ability, and a Musical Instrument can serve as your spellcasting focus.
- Cantrips: You know 2 cantrips at level 1 and learn more at levels shown in the Bard Features table. You can replace one cantrip when you gain a Bard level.
- Prepared spells: Start with four level 1 spells; the number of prepared spells increases with level. You may replace prepared spells when you gain a Bard level.

##### Level 2: Expertise
- Double your proficiency bonus for two skill proficiencies of your choice; at level 9 gain Expertise in two more proficiencies.

##### Level 2: Jack of All Trades
- Add half your Proficiency Bonus (round down) to any ability check that uses a skill you’re not proficient in.

> **A Bard’s Repertoire**
>
> Consider what style of performance your Bard favors (songs, poems, dances, tales). That flavor informs how you describe Bardic features in play.

##### Level 3: Bard Subclass
- Choose a Bard subclass (e.g., College of Lore) that grants features at certain Bard levels.

##### Level 4: Ability Score Improvement
- Gain Ability Score Improvement or a feat at levels 4, 8, 12, and 16.

##### Level 5: Font of Inspiration
- Bardic Inspiration recharges on a Short Rest (and Long Rest). You can also expend a spell slot to regain one use.

##### Level 7: Countercharm
- As a Reaction when you or a creature within 30 feet fails a save vs. Charmed or Frightened, you can cause the save to be rerolled with Advantage.

##### Level 10: Magical Secrets
- When your number of prepared spells increases, you may choose spells from the Bard, Cleric, Druid, and Wizard lists; chosen spells count as Bard spells for you.

##### Level 18: Superior Inspiration
- When you roll Initiative, regain Bardic Inspiration uses until you have two if you have fewer than that.

##### Level 19: Epic Boon
- Gain an Epic Boon feat or other qualifying feat.

##### Level 20: Words of Creation
- Always have Power Word Heal and Power Word Kill prepared; when you cast either, you may target a second creature within 10 feet of the first.

### Bard Subclass — College of Lore

**Plumb the Depths of Magical Knowledge.** College of Lore bards gather spells and secrets from many traditions.

**Level 3: Bonus Proficiencies**
- Gain proficiency in three skills of your choice.

**Level 3: Cutting Words**
- As a Reaction, expend one use of Bardic Inspiration to roll the die and subtract the number from a creature’s damage roll or a successful ability check or attack roll (that creature must be within 60 feet and you must see it).

**Level 6: Magical Discoveries**
- Learn two spells of your choice from the Cleric, Druid, or Wizard lists (cantrips or spells for which you have slots). These spells are always prepared for you and can be changed when you gain Bard levels.

**Level 14: Peerless Skill**
- When you fail an ability check or attack roll, you can expend a Bardic Inspiration die and add it to the d20, possibly turning failure into success; the die is not expended on a failure.
