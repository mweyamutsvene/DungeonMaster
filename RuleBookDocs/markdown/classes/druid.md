# Druid

**Core Druid Traits**

| Primary Ability | Hit Point Die | Saving Throw Proficiencies | Skill Proficiencies |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Wisdom | D8 per Druid level | Intelligence and Wisdom | Choose 2: Arcana, Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Religion, Survival |

**Other core details**
- Weapon Proficiencies: Simple weapons
- Tool Proficiencies: Herbalism Kit
- Armor Training: Light armor and Shields
- Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Leather, Shield, Sickle, Druidic Focus (Quarterstaff), Explorer’s Pack, Herbalism Kit, 9 GP; or (B) 50 GP

Druids belong to ancient orders that call on the forces of nature. They heal, transform into animals, and wield elemental damage. As a Level 1 Druid, you gain Core Druid Traits and the Druid’s level 1 features.

##### Druid Class Features (continued)

##### Level 1: Spellcasting

You have learned to cast spells through studying the mystical forces of nature. See “Spells” for the rules on spellcasting. The information below details how you use those rules with Druid spells, which appear on the Druid spell list later in the class’s description.

**_Cantrips._** You know two cantrips of your choice from the Druid spell list. Druidcraft and Produce Flame are recommended. Whenever you gain a Druid level, you can replace one of your cantrips with another cantrip of your choice. When you reach Druid levels 4 and 10, you learn another cantrip of your choice, as shown in the Druid Features table.

**_Spell Slots._** The Druid Features table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your level 1+ spells. You regain all expended slots when you finish a Long Rest.

**_Prepared Spells of Level 1+._** You prepare the list of level 1+ spells that are available for you to cast with this feature. To start, choose four level 1 spells from the Druid spell list. Animal Friendship, Cure Wounds, Faerie Fire, and Thunderwave are recommended.

**_Changing Your Prepared Spells._** Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can change your list of prepared spells, replacing any of the spells with other Druid spells for which you have spell slots.

**_Spellcasting Ability._** Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for your Druid spells.

**_Spellcasting Focus._** You can use a Druidic Focus as a Spellcasting Focus for your Druid spells.

##### Level 1: Druidic

You know Druidic, the secret language of Druids. While learning this ancient tongue, you also unlocked the magic of communicating with animals; you always have Speak with Animals prepared.

You can use Druidic to leave hidden messages. You and others who know Druidic automatically spot such a message. Others spot the message’s presence with a successful DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check but can’t decipher it without magic.

##### Level 1: Primal Order

You have dedicated yourself to a sacred role.

**Magician.** You know one extra cantrip from the Druid spell list. In addition, you gain a bonus to Intelligence (Arcana or Nature) checks equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum +1).

**Warden.** Gain proficiency with Martial weapons and training with Medium armor.

##### Level 2: Wild Shape

The power of nature allows you to assume the form of an animal. As a Bonus Action, you shape-shift into a Beast form you have learned. You stay in that form for a number of hours equal to half your Druid level or until you use Wild Shape again, become Incapacitated, or die. You can leave the form early as a Bonus Action.

**Number of Uses.** You can use Wild Shape twice. You regain one expended use on a Short Rest and all uses on a Long Rest. You gain additional uses at certain levels as shown in the Druid Features table.

**Known Forms.** You know four Beast forms for this feature, chosen from Beast stat blocks with max CR 1/4 and no Fly Speed (Rat, Riding Horse, Spider, Wolf are recommended). After a Long Rest, you can replace one known form with another eligible form.

When you reach certain levels, your known forms and their max CR increase; at level 8 you can adopt forms with a Fly Speed.

**Rules While Shape‑Shifted.**
- **Temporary Hit Points.** When you assume Wild Shape, you gain Temporary Hit Points equal to your Druid level.
- **Game Statistics.** Your game statistics are replaced by the Beast’s stat block, but you retain your creature type; Hit Points; Hit Dice; Int/Wis/Cha scores; class features; languages; and feats. You keep your proficiencies and use your Proficiency Bonus for them, and you gain the creature’s proficiencies. If the Beast’s modifier is higher, use that one.
- **No Spellcasting.** You can’t cast spells while Wild Shaped, but shape‑shifting doesn’t break Concentration or affect spells you’ve already cast.
- **Objects.** Equipment may fall, merge, or be worn by the new form; DM decides practicality. Merged equipment has no effect.

| Druid Level | Known Forms | Max CR | Fly Speed |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|

##### Level 2: Wild Companion

You can summon a nature spirit as a familiar by expending a spell slot or a use of Wild Shape to cast Find Familiar without Material components. The familiar is Fey and disappears after a Long Rest.

##### Level 3: Druid Subclass

Gain a Druid subclass at level 3 (e.g., Circle of the Land).

##### Level 4: Ability Score Improvement

Gain Ability Score Improvement or a feat at levels 4, 8, 12, and 16.

##### Level 5: Wild Resurgence

Once per turn, if you have no uses of Wild Shape left, you can gain one use by expending a spell slot. You can also expend one use of Wild Shape to gain a level 1 spell slot (once per Long Rest).

##### Level 7: Elemental Fury

Choose one option: Potent Spellcasting (add Wisdom modifier to Druid cantrip damage) or Primal Strike (once per turn when you hit in weapon or Beast form, add 1d8 Cold/Fire/Lightning/Thunder).

##### Level 15: Improved Elemental Fury

Potent Spellcasting: casting a Druid cantrip with range ≥10 ft increases range by 300 ft.

Primal Strike: Primal Strike damage increases to 2d8.

##### Level 18: Beast Spells

While Wild Shaped you can cast spells in Beast form except those with a Material component that has a cost or that consumes the component.

##### Level 19: Epic Boon

Gain an Epic Boon feat or other qualifying feat.

##### Level 20: Archdruid

**Evergreen Wild Shape.** When you roll initiative and have no Wild Shape uses left, regain one use.

**Nature Magician.** Convert uses of Wild Shape into a single spell slot: each use = 2 spell levels (e.g., two uses → level 4 slot). Once used, can’t do so again until a Long Rest.

**Longevity.** You age slower (10 years pass = 1 year of aging).

##### Level 3: Land’s Aid
As a Magic action, expend a use of Wild Shape to create a 10-foot-radius Sphere within 60 feet. Each creature you choose there makes a Constitution save against your spell save DC; on a failed save take 2d6 Necrotic damage (half on success). One chosen creature regains 2d6 Hit Points. Damage and healing increase by 1d6 at Druid levels 10 (3d6) and 14 (4d6).

##### Level 6: Natural Recovery
You can cast one of your prepared Circle spells without expending a spell slot once per Long Rest. After a Short Rest, you can also recover expended spell slots with combined levels equal to up to half your Druid level (round up), none being level 6+, until you finish a Long Rest.

##### Level 10: Nature’s Ward
You are immune to the Poisoned condition and gain resistance to a damage type based on your chosen land (see table below).

| Land Type | Resistance |
|---|---|
| Arid | Fire |
| Polar | Cold |
| Temperate | Lightning |
| Tropical | Acid |

##### Level 14: Nature’s Sanctuary
As a Magic action, you can expend a use of your Wild Shape and cause spectral trees and vines to appear in a 15-foot Cube on the ground within 120 feet of yourself. They last there for 1 minute or until you have the Incapacitated condition or die. You and your allies have Half Cover while in that area, and your allies gain the current Resistance of your Nature’s Ward while there.

As a Bonus Action, you can move the Cube up to 60 feet to ground within 120 feet of yourself.
